This homework only practices Class 8 content: emotions, self-care vocabulary, and present perfect in supportive conversations.

1. Reading

Talking About Stress

Two friends talk about stress and emotions. One friend says she has felt nervous and frustrated recently because work has been overwhelming. She has tried to relax, breathe deeply, and take short breaks during the day.

Later, she talks with a therapist and says she has started to journal and practice self-care more often. She has noticed that these changes have helped her feel calmer, although she still has difficult days.

2. Vocabulary

Match the emotion or self-care phrase to its meaning.

Real-life English tip: When you talk about feelings in English, it often sounds more natural to say I've been feeling stressed or I've started to journal than to use only simple present.

3. Writing

Write 6–8 sentences about how you deal with stress. Use the present perfect and at least four feeling/self-care words from class.

Grammar tip: The present perfect is useful for recent experiences that still matter now: I've felt nervous lately. I've started to journal.
