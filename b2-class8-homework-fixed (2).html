1. Reading

Healthy Habits

Tom has made several lifestyle changes over the past few months. He has joined a gym and has started doing cardio and strength training three times a week. He has also changed his eating habits.

He has stopped skipping breakfast and has prepared healthy lunches for the work week. He says his energy level has improved, and exercise has helped reduce his stress. He believes the best way to change your lifestyle is to start with small changes and build gradually.

2. Vocabulary

Match the health and wellness expression to its meaning.

Real-life English tip: In English, people often talk about healthy habits with the present perfect when the change started before now and still matters now.

3. Writing

Write 6–8 sentences about healthy changes you have made or want to make. Use the present perfect several times.

Words: 0

Grammar tip: Use have/has + past participle: I have started, She has changed.

4. Speaking

Speak for 30–45 seconds about healthy habits or lifestyle changes. Use the present perfect.
